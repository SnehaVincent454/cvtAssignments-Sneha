Poppping Mechanism:

Each kernel of popcorn contain a certain amount of moisture and oil. Unlike most other grains, the outer hull of the popcorn kernel is both strong and impervious to mositure and the starch inside ocnsists almost entirely of a hard type. as the oil and water within the kernel are heated, they then turnthe moisture in the kernel into pressurized steam continues to increase until the breaking point of the hull is reached. Popcorn can be cooked with buttter or oil.ALthough small quantities can bee popped in a stove-top kettle or pot in a home kitchen, commercial sale of freshly popped popcorn employs specially designed popcorn machines, which were invented in Chicaho, Illinois, by CharlesCretors in 1885. Terminology:

in the popcorn inductry, a popped kernal is known as "flakes".Two shapes of flakes are commercially important. "Butterfly" (or "Snowfalkes") flakes are irregular in shape and have a number of proturding "wings"."mushroom"flakes are largeely ball-shaped. with few wings. Butterfly flakes are regarded as having better mouthfeel, with greater tenderness and less noticable hulls. Mushroom flakes are less fragile then butterfly flakes and are therefore often used for packaged popcorn or confectionary, such as caramel corn.
